Sam: The basic idea is to blend reactive alignment with a predictive term that estimates the future velocities of neighboring agents. This is done using a short-term prediction model that takes into account the current state and motion of the neighbors. The blending parameter allows the system to interpolate between purely reactive and anticipatory behaviors.

Sam: The authors performed extensive simulations to compare the performance of FDA flocking with a purely reactive baseline model. The results show that FDA flocking achieves faster and higher alignment, as well as improved robustness to sensing and communication delays, and measurement noise.
